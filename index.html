Expert Advice

Moving Tips From the Pros

Field-tested advice from Team Pro Moving to make your move day smoother, faster, and stress-free.

01Book 2–4 Weeks Out — Best Slots Fill Fast

▼

The best time slots — especially weekends and end-of-month dates — fill up quickly. Reserve your movers at least 2–4 weeks in advance to lock in your preferred date and avoid last-minute headaches. Same-day slots are available but always limited.

02Pack an Essentials Box Last — Load It First

▼

Pack a "first night" box with toiletries, a change of clothes, phone charger, snacks, and medications. Pack it last so it goes on the truck first — that way it comes off first and goes straight into your new home without hunting through boxes.

03Label Every Box — Top AND Sides

▼

Label each box on the top and at least two sides. When boxes are stacked in the truck or storage, only the sides are visible. Include the destination room AND a brief content list. It saves hours of confusion on unload day and helps our crew place boxes exactly where you want them.

04Use Small Boxes for Heavy Items

▼

Books, tools, and canned goods should always go in small boxes. Large boxes packed with heavy items are dangerous and hard to carry. Keep heavy things in small, manageable boxes and fill large boxes with lightweight items like pillows, linens, and stuffed animals.

05Never Mix Rooms in the Same Box

▼

One room per box — always. Mixing rooms creates confusion when unpacking and slows down our crew when placing boxes. Use color-coded labels by room for even faster unloading. A little organization before move day saves hours on the other side.

06Defrost Your Refrigerator the Night Before

▼

Turn off your refrigerator and freezer the night before your move and leave the doors open. This prevents water damage during transport, makes the unit lighter and easier to move, and protects your floors at both locations.

07Photograph Electronics Before Disassembly

▼

Before disconnecting your TV setup, gaming system, or home office — snap photos of the cable connections. When you're reassembling at the new place, you'll thank yourself for those reference shots. Also photograph furniture backs before disassembly so reassembly is fast.

08Clear a Path Before We Arrive

▼

A clear path from each room to the front door means faster, safer loading. Move small items, rugs, and floor obstacles the night before. Reserve parking close to your entrance for the moving truck. The more open the path, the faster your move — and the lower your final bill.
